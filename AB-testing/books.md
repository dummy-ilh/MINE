
### 📚 The Resource Shortlist

#### **1. A/B Testing (The Gold Standard)**
* **Primary:** *Trustworthy Online Controlled Experiments* (Kohavi, Tang, Xu).
    * *Why:* The "Bible" used by FAANG interviewers. Covers Power Analysis, Novelty Effects, and Guardrail Metrics.
* **Quick Reference:** [ExperimentGuide.com](https://experimentguide.com) (Companion site with free chapters).
* **Technical Deep-Dive:** *Statistical Methods in Online A/B Testing* (Georgi Georgiev).
    * *Why:* Best for Frequentist vs. Bayesian nuances.

#### **2. Product Sense & Metrics**
* **Frameworks:** *Cracking the PM Interview* (McDowell).
    * *Why:* Teaches the **GAME** framework (Goal → Actions → Metrics → Evaluate) used to diagnose metric drops.
* **DS-Specific Practice:** *Ace the Data Science Interview* (Singh & Huo).
    * *Why:* 201 real questions from Meta/Amazon/Google with step-by-step solutions.

#### **3. High-Signal Free Content**
* **Evan Miller’s Blog:** Read *"How Not to Run an A/B Test"* (essential for understanding the "peeking" problem).
* **Engineering Blogs:** **Netflix Tech Blog** & **Meta Engineering** (to see how these concepts scale).
* **Interactive Practice:** [InterviewQuery.com](https://interviewquery.com) (for real-world question banks).
* **Intuition:** *Probably Overthinking It* (Substack by Allen Downey).

### 💡 Interview Checklist (What they are looking for)
* [ ] **The "Peeking" Problem:** Do you know why checking results early ruins the p-value?
* [ ] **Network Effects:** How do you test a feature on a social network where users interact?
* [ ] **Guardrail Metrics:** If "Time Spent" goes up but "Customer Support Tickets" also double, is it a win?
* [ ] **Twyman's Law:** "Any statistic that looks interesting or different is usually wrong."
