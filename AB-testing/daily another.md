## Day 1: The "Improve" Question (Easy)

**Question:** "How would you improve Instagram for senior citizens (ages 65+)?"

### 1. Clarify & Set Goals
First, why do this? For Meta, the goal is likely **Engagement** and **Retention**. If we can get seniors onto the platform, we increase the total user base and keep families connected.

### 2. Identify User Pain Points
Let's look at why a 70-year-old might struggle with the current app:
* **Accessibility:** Small font sizes and low-contrast icons make navigation difficult.
* **Feature Overload:** The UI is cluttered with Reels, Stories, Shop, and DMs. It’s "noisy."
* **Safety/Privacy Fears:** High anxiety about who can see their photos or falling for scams.
* **Content Relevance:** The algorithm might show them trending Gen-Z memes instead of family updates.

### 3. Prioritize a Segment
We’ll focus on the **"Grandparent User"**—someone whose primary motivation is seeing photos of their grandkids but feels overwhelmed by the tech.

### 4. Proposed Solutions
* **"Legacy Mode" Toggle:** A simplified interface setting that hides the "Shop" and "Reels" tabs, enlarges icons, and increases font contrast.
* **Family-First Feed:** A dedicated tab or "Close Friends" filter that ensures family posts appear at the top, bypassing the "suggested content" noise.
* **Scam Protection Shields:** A feature that automatically flags DMs from non-contacts with a "Verify Identity" prompt to reduce anxiety.

### 5. Success Metrics (The "North Star")
How do we know we won? We track the **7-day retention rate** for users 65+. If they keep coming back every week, the improvements worked.
